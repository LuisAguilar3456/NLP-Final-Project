- It takes 4 to 6 weeks for the body to fully adapt to the Iron Man distance.
- Training in the last 2 weeks can ruin the fitness gained from the previous 4 to 6 weeks.
- It is important to give the body time to rest and recover during the tapering period.
- Short, quick efforts can be done during this phase.
- Weight training should be avoided in the last 2-4 weeks unless consistent throughout training.
- Volume should be gradually decreased leading up to the race, typically around 50% of peak volume in the last 2 weeks.
- Every individual tapers differently and it may take some trial and error to find the right tapering strategy.
- Nutrition is important during training and races to avoid issues like GI problems.
- Practice nutrition strategies during training to find what works best for you.
- Having redundancies in nutrition during races is important in case anything is lost or there are unexpected circumstances.
- Salt balance is crucial for proper carbohydrate absorption, so it is important to have the right amount of salt intake for your needs.
- Too much salt can also cause issues, so it is important to find the right balance through training.
